Apply for a protection order

Before you begin

  • We will ask questions about your situation — what happened and when — what protection you need. This online interview can take an hour or more to complete.
  • At the end, you will have the necessary forms to take to court. Protection orders may take 4 weeks or longer. We will ask if you need a temporary protection order to protect you while waiting for the court to hear your case.
  • You can apply yourself. You can also ask a friend or lawyer to apply on your behalf. In some situations, the police may apply for you.

Conditions sought in the order

A court making a domestic violence order must impose a condition that {{ dv1.respondent.name.the }} —
Be of good behaviour towards {{ dv1.aggrieved.name.the }} and not commit domestic violence against {{ dv1.aggrieved.name.the }}.

If the order includes a named person who is an adult —
Be of good behaviour towards the named person and not commit associated domestic violence against the named person.

If the order includes a named person who is a child —
Be of good behaviour towards the child and not commit associated domestic violence against the child and not expose the child to domestic violence.

A court may also impose any other condition that the court considers necessary in the circumstances and desirable in the interests of {{ dv1.aggrieved.name.the }}, any named person or {{ dv1.respondent.name.the }}.
