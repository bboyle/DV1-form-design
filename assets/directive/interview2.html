Preparing for court

Prepare your application for a protection order

Before you begin

  • This online form can take an hour or more to complete. You can apply yourself. You can also ask a friend or lawyer to apply on your behalf. In some situations, the police may apply for you.
  • You may find answering these questions difficult because we will ask questions about your situation—what happened and when—what protection you need.
  • At the end of this guide you will have the necessary forms (possibly 2 or 3) to take to court.
  • Once you lodge the forms at court, the person you are wanting protection from will get a copy of your application.

Going to court

  • You need to lodge the forms and all attachments at a magistrates court. You can do this yourself, or you can ask a friend or family member to do so on your behalf.
  • You will be given a date to appear in court (usually in about 4 weeks time).
  • Your court date can be sooner if you also ask for an urgent temporary protection order. You have to explain why you need this.
  • The person you want to be protected from will have to appear in court at the same time.

What conditions are on a protection order?

All protection orders have two compulsory conditions which say that:

  1. The respondent must be of good behaviour towards the aggrieved and not commit domestic violence
  2. The respondent must be of good behaviour towards any named person and not commit associated domestic violence against the named person.

You can ask the court to include in extra conditions.

Serving the application

Court registry staff will give a copy to the Queensland Police Service and they will make sure {{ interview.respondent.name.short }} is served. {{ interview.respondent.name.short }} must be served with a copy of the application.

What happens at court?

The court will make an order if it accepts:

  • there has been an act of domestic violence, and
  • you and the respondent are in one of the relationships covered by the law, and
  • the court thinks a domestic violence order is necessary or desirable in your situation.
